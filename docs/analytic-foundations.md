# Analytic Foundations

PSYCHE/OS is not grounded in one school. It is a composite interpretive system.

The project borrows from several traditions because each one explains a different part of the signal:

- psychology explains motives, defenses, patterns, and symbolic life
- sociology explains role, status, field pressure, and habit
- anthropology explains rituals, symbols, meaning systems, and liminal transitions
- cognitive science explains how tools and environments become part of cognition
- technology studies explain how interfaces, tooling, and infrastructure shape attention and agency

These are foundations for interpretation, not proofs of validity.

## Core Lenses

### Analytical Psychology

Used for:
- archetypal language
- shadow dynamics
- symbolic compensation
- individuation-like tensions between persona and deeper structure

Reference:
- Carl Jung overview: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Carl-Jung

### Personology And Narrative Identity

Used for:
- reading personality as a whole system rather than isolated traits
- treating life as an evolving narrative with chapters, tensions, and identity revisions

References:
- Henry Murray / personology: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Henry-Murray
- McAdams on narrative identity: https://www.scholars.northwestern.edu/en/publications/narrative-identity-what-is-it-what-does-it-do-how-do-you-measure-

### Sociology Of Practice

Used for:
- habitus
- field-specific role performance
- legitimacy, recognition, and status pressures
- the gap between declared identity and enacted behavior

References:
- Pierre Bourdieu overview: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Pierre-Bourdieu
- Habitus reference: https://academic.oup.com/edited-volume/28103/chapter/212230995

### Interpretive Anthropology

Used for:
- thick description
- symbolic systems
- rituals and liminality
- reading behavior inside a local meaning-world instead of as decontextualized traits

References:
- Geertz thick description archive: https://archive.icann.org/en/ombudsman/clifford-geertz.htm
- Victor Turner overview: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Victor-Turner

### Extended Cognition And Affordances

Used for:
- treating tools, interfaces, notebooks, agents, and workflows as parts of cognition
- analyzing how environments invite, constrain, or offload action

References:

## Practical Consequence For Prompt Design

These foundations imply that good prompts must:

- preserve behavior and context before interpretation
- compare sources instead of trusting one source family too much
- capture contradictions, not just flattering patterns
- model the technological environment as part of the psyche, not just as metadata
- end in a vector of movement, not a pile of disconnected insights

If a prompt cannot do those things, it is not strong enough for PSYCHE/OS.
